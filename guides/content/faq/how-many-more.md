# Calculating Pokémon: A Basic Guide to How-Many-More

This website is a little different than the other Calculators out there. This one tells you the probability of how many more you need to catch in order to find something better than the one you have in your hand.

https://sites.google.com/view/how-many-more

https://www.how-many-more.com/how-to-use-this-calculator

Some common settings I use:

- Pokemon: Use the stage of the pokemon you're catching, usually the basic stage as that's the one most accurate with the Friendship Medal system (bronze, silver, gold levels change depending on the stage)
- Comparison Level: I judge at lvl 50 as 75 is too far to be considered at this time. Notice the lack of lvl 60 here; That's because these calcs are ing list-agnostic, covering only subskills and nature
- Guaranteed Number of Gold Skills: depends on your Friendship Level or corresponding friendship medal settings for this pokemon (0 for bronze vs 1 for silver vs 2 for gold)
- Subseeds: Use/Don't use subseeds depending on your premium/f2p status
- Specialty: Select the right specialty, duh! (Charge strength mons are hybrids between berries and skills so this site doesn't calculate how well they do well)
- Ingredient List: Important on ing mons ONLY so make sure you look at the odds for AAA/AAx/ABB when calculating for them, whatever is relevant to your ing mon. Set to don't care for berry/skill mon.

Advanced Settings:

- Comparison Mode: Always look for better (why would I want the same performance?)
- Sneaky Snack: Toggling this on means the calc will ignore Ing Finders/Skill Triggers, off considers them otherwise
- Friendship Level: Another alternative to the Guaranteed Number of Gold Skills option
- Helping Bonus: If I absolutely want HB on my pokemon (like my healer for example), I will set the HB target of the _target_ pokemon to be the highest value (full team output), otherwise I leave this setting alone. If the pokemon you caught has HB, and you really care about having it on any future catches, I recommend you update the HB values of both the pokemon you have and the target pokemon to the highest value.

## Advanced Tips

**How to calculate ACROSS pokemon species**

Let's say you've found a decent Pawmi and you're unsure whether to invest in it or not. You're also aware that Pawmi has a counterpart that does it's job slightly better (Gardevoir) so you'd like to know the odds of finding better with that pokemon as well.

Use your favorite Calculator (take a look at the other Calculator posts in the FAQ), punch in your Pawmi's stats (as a Pawmot at lvl 50/60) and punch in a Gardevoir trying to find an equivalent stat spread that the Gardevoir can match it on in terms of what you're calculating for (number of procs per day for skill mons, or number of ingredients for ingredient mons).

In my example, I have a Pawmi with HB, STM, and a Speed Up nature, proccing 4.91 triggers a day. An equivalent Gard would be one with HB, HSM, and a Trigger Up nature. I want my Gard to have HB as well so I used a spread that included it. You now have the appropriate stats needed on a Gard to beat, which you can now punch into How-Many-More and see how many more you need to catch of either Pawmi or Ralts to see the possibilities of finding better. Since Pawmi upgrades its Friendship Badge from bronze to silver after catching 10, I did calcs for both to see what my chances were for bronze badge and silver badge. (Each Pawmi at bronze will have ~1% chance of being better than the one I caught, while a silver badge Pawmi will have ~1.8% chance)

Some notes:

- My Friendship Badge level for each species is different so I made sure to accurately reflect that in the How-Many-More calc.
- I want a guaranteed HB on my healer so I made sure to give it a higher weight under advanced options, both to the pokemon I have and to the pokemon I'm searching for.
